About Siargao

Siargao is composed of 48 islands and divided into 9 municipalities. Siargao Islands contains the largest mangrove forest reserves in Mindanao, at Del Carmen.The Mindanao current, which flows westward via the Siargao Strait, amplifies the effects of the winds and currents flowing continuously from the Pacific Ocean on the Siargao Islands.

The coral and rock reefs that face the Pacific on the island are located on the edge of the Philippine Trench, and the extremely deep offshore waters ensure that the ocean swells hit them with full force. Excellent surfing conditions can be found in Siargao, especially from August to November during the southwest "habagat" monsoon when the predominant wind is offshore.

Why you should visit Siargao

Unlike any other tourists spots in the Philippines, Siargao is not crowded yet that is why if you don't like crowded places now is the time you should visit Siargao. Enjoy and unwind with the Natural Reserves located in Siargao. (Sugba Lagoon, Sohoton Cove and Bucas Grande). Your staycation in Siargao is not complete withouth trying to surf at Siargao, it is not called 'Surfing Capital of the Philippines' if it's not the perfect place to surf. Experience a wonderful nightlife during your stay because jungle bars in Siargao and other bars, exists! Perfect for picture takings, every angle is just perfect with the beautiful background.
